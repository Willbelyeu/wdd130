Intro to crafting stone arrowheads

history of arrows

“If you are like me, you find how our ancestors did things very interesting. As a child, I wondered how they could make arrowheads out of stone. In order to help others learn what I have I put this together to explain a few materials and how you can work them.”

“The first thing that you need is a material to work with. The three I have used are glass, quartz, and obsidian. Click these links for more information about each. The only tools you should need is a point off a deer antler or a large nail, a piece of either leather or some such cut resistant pad, and a work area that can easily be cleaned.”

Context of glass in arrow-making

“Glass is often used nowadays to learn how to make arrowheads due to its availability. All you need to do is find a beer bottle, put a nail in it, plug the top with your thumb, and shake it up and down a few times. The bottom will come off and you should have enough material for two or three arrowheads.”

“Glass is easy to work because it breaks very predictably and easily. All you need to do is set your tool to the edge and press hard and a flake will come off.”

“There are a few drawbacks to using glass, one such is that it is very brittle so the heads made from it damage easily. Also it isn’t a very historic material. Still, it is a good training resource as you can learn methods you will use on other stones.”
